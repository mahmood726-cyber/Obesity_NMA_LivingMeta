## E156 Abstract (CURRENT BODY)

Among adults with obesity, how do tirzepatide 15 mg, semaglutide 2.4 mg, and orforglipron 36 mg compare for achieving clinically meaningful weight loss? We synthesized five placebo-controlled RCTs (NCT04184622, NCT04657003, NCT03548935, NCT03552757, NCT05051579) covering 5,490 participants in a star-network NMA with placebo as common comparator. For >=5% body weight loss, estimated RR vs placebo: tirzepatide 15 mg 2.63 (95% CrI 2.36–2.94), semaglutide 2.4 mg 2.40 (95% CrI 2.18–2.64), orforglipron 36 mg 7.75 (95% CrI 3.38–17.8; Phase 2 only, n=108). In indirect comparison, tirzepatide ranked first (P-score 0.87), semaglutide second (0.62), orforglipron uncertain pending Phase 3 data. All three agents substantially outperform placebo; tirzepatide shows numerically superior >=5% responder rates, consistent with the dual GIP/GLP-1 mechanism. Direct head-to-head trials (SURMOUNT-5 recruiting) are needed before clinical superiority claims can be made.
